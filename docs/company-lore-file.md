Mission, Vision & Values
Mission: Coach House exists to empower community‑rooted leaders by providing operational
infrastructure and strategic support that turn passion into effective, sustainable impact.
Vision: A world where community passion is backed by the clarity and support needed to build
organizations that are stable, impactful and built to thrive.
Values: Clarity over complexity, sustainability, founder dignity, operational excellence, data‑informed
learning, collaboration over competition, and valuing leaders’ well‑being.
Need Statement: Many nonprofit founders launch with deep connection to their community but
without access to systems, training or infrastructure. Nearly two‑thirds of U.S. nonprofits operate on
annual budgets under $500,000 and nearly half have no financial cushion. Without integrated
systems and strategic frameworks, promising organizations struggle to move beyond survival mode
.
Founding Story & Timeline
Coach House did not appear overnight; it emerged from decades of community‑based leadership. Founders
Joel  and  Paula  Hamernick spent  more  than  25  years  rebuilding  organizations  and  pioneering  social
enterprises. The timeline below highlights major milestones leading to Coach House’s formation.

Behind their 115‑year‑old graystone home on
Chicago’s South Side stood an equally old brick coach
house. Built around 1900 as a stable and hay loft, it
later became a small apartment. When Joel and Paula

space and Joel rebuilt it with help from their children,

The Symbol of the Coach House
Physical inspiration: The company’s name arises from the small two‑story coach house behind the
Hamernick family’s 115‑year‑old graystone. Once a horse stable and hay loft, the structure had
deteriorated; only the exterior bricks remained. Paula designed and Joel rebuilt the space—with help
from their children—transforming it into a bright, cozy apartment.
Metaphor: This rebuilding process—honoring a structure’s legacy while creating something new—
mirrors the Hamernicks’ approach to nonprofit revitalization. The coach house stands as a tangible
reminder that with vision, perseverance and skilled craftsmanship, decaying structures (or
organizations) can be renewed.

Approach: Joel emphasizes aligning capital, governance and strategy over long time horizons,
focusing on durable partnerships rather than transactional fundraising. He blends high‑level
strategy with practical implementation, helping organizations clarify purpose, strengthen
governance and develop credible growth pathways.
Personal: Joel and Paula have seven children and three granddaughters and live in Chicago’s
Woodlawn community.

Coach House Today: What It Does
Services & Programs
Coach House offers a suite of services designed to help nonprofits transition from passion to impact:
Solution‑oriented executive advising: With over 25 years of nonprofit leadership experience,
Coach House provides consulting to clarify mission, strengthen strategy and build systems so
organizations thrive. Advising covers fundraising, network‑building, interim leadership and
problem solving.
Idea‑to‑Impact Accelerator: A nine‑week program that guides early‑stage nonprofit leaders
through mission definition, systems thinking, program piloting, budgets, fundraising,
communications, board governance and optional workshops in legal structure, operations, branding
and grant writing. Cohorts include in‑person and online sessions and deliver a tested program,
strategic roadmap, evaluation system, fundraising pitch and board strategy.
Networking & Access: Coach House helps leaders build networks across practitioners,
philanthropists, academics and concerned citizens.
Fiscal sponsorship (Model C): Provides organizations with 501(c)(3) coverage so they can focus on
mission while leveraging Coach House’s tax‑exempt status.
Coach House Conversations: Curated gatherings where diverse leaders discuss visions for a more
equitable and flourishing Chicago while building relationships and mutual understanding.
Leadership development: Mentoring based on Rare Leadership, Emotional Intelligence and Servant
Leadership to nurture healthy leaders and teams.
The Platform: An online platform offering asynchronous learning, member forums, operations
support and verified sustainability. It aims to make high‑quality infrastructure and expert guidance
accessible to early‑stage nonprofits at affordable rates.

Theory of Change
Coach House operates on a simple but powerful theory of change: If community‑based nonprofit leaders
are provided with expert advising, structured training and accessible operational tools, they will gain
the  capacity  to  develop  sound  strategies,  design  sustainable  programs,  manage  finances  and
evaluate impact. Stronger organizations will deliver clearer missions, stable systems, effective leadership
and measurable results, ultimately expanding impact and deepening community trust.
Why Coach House Matters
Small and early‑stage nonprofits often face a fragmented landscape of compliance requirements, software
tools, fundraising expectations and governance demands. Without integrated systems, they spend years
reacting  rather  than  building.  Coach  House  fills  this  gap  by  combining  experienced  mentorship with
accessible infrastructure, enabling leaders to build organizations that endure.
Through  decades  of  grassroots  experience—from  rebuilding  a  camp  in  rural  Michigan  to  relocating  a
ministry out of Cabrini‑Green, launching social enterprises and leading university civic engagement—Joel
and Paula Hamernick have learned to honour legacy while forging new paths. The restored coach house
behind  their  home  serves  as  both  metaphor  and  namesake  for  their  work:  rebuilding  what  has  been
neglected and creating welcoming spaces where new possibilities can flourish.
