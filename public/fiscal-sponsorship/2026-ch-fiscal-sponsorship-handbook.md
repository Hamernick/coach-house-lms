# FS: How this works

# **Our Fiscal Sponsorship Model**

Coach House fiscally sponsors community-based, civic, and mission-driven projects that are charitable in nature, primarily noncommercial, and aligned with a clear public benefit.

Our fiscal sponsorship program is structured as a grantor–grantee relationship, sometimes referred to by the IRS as a Model C fiscal sponsorship.

**The Model:** 

* Coach House serves as the fiscal sponsor (the grantor)

* Your project or organization serves as the sponsored entity (the grantee)

Donors, foundations, corporations, or public entities make charitable contributions to Coach House for the benefit of your approved project. Coach House then makes charitable grants to you to support project-related expenses.

This structure allows projects without their own 501(c)(3) status to:

* Receive tax-deductible donations

* Apply for many grants

* Establish financial credibility

* Operate with appropriate oversight and compliance

## **Why Fiscal Sponsorship Matters**

Many foundations and institutional funders are legally prohibited from making grants directly to individuals or organizations that do not have their own tax-exempt status.

Fiscal sponsorship bridges this gap by allowing Coach House to:

* Receive funds on behalf of sponsored projects  
* Hold those funds in a restricted account  
* Grant funds to projects in support of approved charitable activities

This enables early-stage and emerging initiatives to raise funds responsibly without rushing into forming a standalone nonprofit.

**Ownership & Control**

While fiscally sponsored:

* You retain ownership and control over your project’s work and operations  
* Coach House does not direct your programs or manage day-to-day activities  
* Coach House provides financial oversight, compliance, and guardrails required by law

Fiscal sponsorship is not a partnership, joint venture, or employment relationship—it is a professional grant relationship designed to protect both parties.

**How Fundraising Works**

Fiscal sponsorship provides access and credibility—but fundraising remains your responsibility.

Sponsored projects are expected to:

* Identify and cultivate donors  
* Build relationships with funders  
* Lead outreach and fundraising efforts

Coach House supports this work by:

* Providing guidance on compliant fundraising  
* Offering templates, tools, and best practices  
* Being available to answer questions and review materials  
* Connecting projects to capacity-building resources when appropriate

**Non-Exclusive Sponsorship**

Coach House’s fiscal sponsorship is non-exclusive.

In some cases, projects may choose to work with more than one fiscal sponsor—for example, if a funder requires a sponsor based in a specific geography or with specialized eligibility.

Projects are responsible for ensuring that multiple sponsorship arrangements do not conflict.

**Contributed vs. Earned Revenue**

Understanding revenue types is essential under fiscal sponsorship.

### Contributed Revenue

* Donations and grants given freely  
* No goods or services provided in return  
* Eligible for tax deduction (subject to IRS rules)  
* Accepted by Coach House for sponsored projects

### Earned Revenue

* Payments for goods or services  
* Generally not tax-deductible  
* Typically should be received directly by your organization or entity

In limited cases, partially deductible contributions may be accepted, subject to review and approval.

**In Summary**

Coach House fiscal sponsorship provides:

* A compliant fundraising pathway  
* Institutional credibility  
* Financial oversight and accountability  
* Space to test, grow, and refine your work

The sponsee project or organization brings:

* The idea  
* The leadership  
* The fundraising effort  
* The commitment to public benefit

Together, this model supports impact with integrity.

# FS: What we look for

## **What We Look For**

Our fiscal sponsorship program is non-selective by discipline and welcomes projects across a wide range of community and civic focus areas that are aligned with our charitable purpose and values.

To be approved for fiscal sponsorship, your project must clearly demonstrate that it meets the following criteria:

* **Project activities serve a clear community or public benefit**  
   Activities should advance community support, development, education, health, economic opportunity, civic engagement, or related charitable purposes.

* **Activities are charitable in nature**  
   The project must be consistent with IRS Section 501(c)(3) requirements and structured to benefit a charitable class or the public at large.

* **The project is not primarily commercial**  
  While projects may include earned revenue components, the primary purpose may not be private profit, commercial gain, or personal enrichment.

An application for fiscal sponsorship will be denied if it does not meet one or more of these core criteria. Specifically, applications may be denied if:

* The project’s charitable purpose or community benefit is not clearly defined  
* The activities appear to be primarily commercial or for personal gain  
* The project does not align with Coach House’s mission, core values, or risk standards

**We Cannot Sponsor Projects That:**

* Function primarily as **re-granting entities**, including award programs, grant programs, or scholarship programs that distribute funds to third parties

* Are **commercial ventures** with the primary goal of generating profit

* Lack a clearly articulated charitable purpose or community benefit

* Present legal, financial, or compliance risks that cannot be reasonably managed under fiscal sponsorship

**Our Review Process**

We aim to be thoughtful, transparent, and inclusive.

All fiscal sponsorship applications are reviewed by Coach House staff prior to approval. If there are questions, gaps, or concerns about your application, our team will reach out to request clarification or additional information before a final decision is made.

Final approval is granted following internal review and, where required, approval by Coach House leadership or governing bodies.

# FS: Requirements

## **Fiscal Sponsorship Requirements**

Coach House fiscal sponsorship is designed to support **community-based initiatives, civic projects, and mission-driven programs** that are ready to raise funds responsibly under a 501(c)(3) umbrella. To ensure strong stewardship, compliance, and alignment, all sponsored projects must meet the requirements below.

**Application & Eligibility Requirements**

To be considered for fiscal sponsorship with Coach House, you must:

* **Be engaged with Coach House**  
   Fiscal sponsorship is available to projects that are members of the Coach House community or have participated in Coach House programs (such as the Accelerator, Commons, or an approved intake pathway).

* **Submit a complete application**  
   Applicants must complete and submit the fiscal sponsorship application, including a clear project description, leadership information, preliminary budget, and intended fundraising activities.

* **Operate for a charitable purpose**  
   Your project must serve a clear public benefit and be charitable in nature, consistent with Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code.

* **Be non-commercial**  
   Projects may include earned revenue components, but the primary purpose may not be private profit or commercial gain.

* **Align with Coach House’s mission, core values, and risk standards**  
   Approval is based on alignment with our mission and core values, project readiness, compliance considerations, and demonstrated capacity to manage funds responsibly.

**Requirements for Managing Fiscal Sponsorship**

To successfully manage a fiscal sponsorship relationship, you must:

* Have a **valid U.S. mailing address**  
* Maintain a **working email address** that you check regularly  
* Have **regular access to the internet**  
* Be able to communicate and submit required information **in writing** when requested

Fiscal sponsorship is primarily managed through Coach House’s online systems and email correspondence. Certain documents (such as tax forms or returned donation checks) may be sent via U.S. mail.

**Providing Your Tax ID Number**

As part of the application process, you must identify the legal entity and U.S. tax identification number associated with the sponsored project for tax reporting purposes.

Coach House can fiscally sponsor projects operating under the following **U.S.-based legal structures**, provided they have a valid U.S. tax ID:

* Individual / Sole Proprietor (using a Social Security Number)  
* Informal Group with an Employer Identification Number (EIN)  
* Limited Liability Company (LLC)  
* Corporation  
* Partnership

The tax ID provided will be used for required IRS reporting related to grant distributions.

**International Organizations**

At this time, **Coach House does not accept projects or organizations that are based outside of the United States**.

To be eligible for fiscal sponsorship, projects must:

* Be based in the United States  
* Have a valid U.S. tax identification number  
* Have a U.S. mailing address

This policy may be revisited in the future, but fiscal sponsorship is currently limited to U.S.-based projects only.

**Important Notes**

* Fiscal sponsorship is a **grantor–grantee relationship**, not a partnership, joint venture, or employment arrangement.  
* Sponsored projects must comply with all Coach House fiscal sponsorship policies, which may be updated with notice.  
* Certain activities—including partisan political activity and unapproved lobbying—are prohibited.  
* Coach House reserves sole discretion over approval, continuation, and termination of fiscal sponsorship.

# **FUNDRAISING APPROVAL & DISCLOSURE POLICY**

## **1\. Prior Approval Requirement**

Under the terms of the Fiscal Sponsorship Grant Agreement, **all fundraising solicitations, grant applications, sponsorship requests, donation pages, and related materials referencing Coach House Solutions Group must be reviewed and approved in writing by Coach House staff prior to submission or publication.**

This requirement applies to:

* Foundation and corporate grant proposals

* Government grant applications

* Individual donor solicitations

* Sponsorship requests

* Crowdfunding campaigns

* Event fundraising materials

* Website donation pages

* Pitch decks referencing tax-deductible status

* Any document stating or implying charitable deductibility

No materials may be distributed, submitted, or published until written approval is received from Coach House.

**2\. Rationale**

Coach House Solutions Group (“Sponsor”) is a 501(c)(3) public charity and retains:

* Legal and fiduciary responsibility over all restricted funds

* Variance power and discretion over grantmaking

* Authority over charitable solicitations conducted in its name

Pre-approval ensures:

* Compliance with IRS regulations

* Alignment with Sponsor policies

* Accurate representation of the fiscal sponsorship relationship

* Protection of both Sponsor and Grantee

Failure to obtain prior approval may constitute a material breach of the Fiscal Sponsorship Grant Agreement.

**3\. Required Disclosure Language**

All approved fundraising materials must clearly describe the fiscal sponsorship relationship. The following sample language may be used:

### **Short Version (for decks, websites, email signatures)**

\[Project Name\] is fiscally sponsored by Coach House Solutions Group, a 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization. All donations are tax-deductible to the extent permitted by law and are made to Coach House Solutions Group in support of the \[Project Name\].

**Medium Version (for grant proposals)**

\[Project Name\] operates under a grantor–grantee fiscal sponsorship agreement (Model C) with Coach House Solutions Group, an Illinois nonprofit corporation recognized as tax-exempt under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code. Coach House receives and administers charitable contributions designated for the Project and makes grants to support Project activities in furtherance of its charitable mission.

**Government Grant Version (if applicable)**

\[Project Name\] is fiscally sponsored by Coach House Solutions Group, a 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization that maintains full discretion and control over all funds received in support of the Project in accordance with IRS regulations governing charitable organizations.

**4\. Prohibited Representations**

Grantee may not:

* Represent itself as a 501(c)(3) organization

* State that it is a “nonprofit” unless it has independent legal status

* Use Coach House EIN without written authorization

* Make commitments on behalf of Coach House

* Imply automatic approval or pass-through of funds

All funds are charitable assets of Coach House and subject to Sponsor discretion.

**5\. Submission Process**

To request approval, Grantee must submit:

* Final draft of materials (PDF or link)

* Funder name and deadline (if applicable)

* Requested budget amount

* Proposed use of funds

Approval will be provided in writing.

# FS: CH Internal Controls

# **Coach House Fiscal Sponsorship**

## **Internal Financial Controls & Grant Disbursement Process**

This document outlines Coach House’s **internal financial controls, record‑keeping, and grant‑making procedures** for fiscally sponsored projects (Model C) once an application has been approved and a Fiscal Sponsorship Grant Agreement is fully executed.

The purpose of this process is to ensure:

* Compliance with IRS 501(c)(3) requirements  
* Clear separation of sponsor and project responsibilities  
* Accurate financial tracking and reporting  
* Strong stewardship of donor funds  
* Consistency across all sponsored projects

**I. Post‑Approval Setup (Pre‑Donation)**

Once a project is approved and the grant agreement is signed:

### **1\. Project Setup (Internal)**

Coach House will:

* Assign a **unique project ID / restricted fund code**  
* Create a **restricted fund ledger** within the accounting system  
* Link the project to:  
  * Approved scope of work  
  * Authorized signatories  
  * Approved fundraising channels  
* Store executed agreement and application in the project’s secure file

### **2\. Project Onboarding (External)**

Coach House will provide the grantee with:

* Written confirmation of sponsorship approval  
* Fundraising and donor communication guidelines  
* Instructions for directing donations (checks, ACH, online links)  
* Spending request and documentation requirements

No fundraising may begin until onboarding is complete.

**II. When a Donation Is Received**

### **1\. Receipt of Funds**

Donations may arrive via:

* Online giving platform  
* ACH / wire transfer  
* Check payable to Coach House  
* Approved third‑party fundraising tools

All donations **must be made payable to Coach House** and reference the sponsored project.

### **2\. Initial Review & Acceptance**

Coach House finance staff will:

* Verify donor intent and project designation  
* Confirm the donation complies with sponsorship policies  
* Screen for prohibited sources or restricted activities  
* Accept or decline the donation at Coach House’s discretion

### **3\. Deposit & Recording**

Upon acceptance:

* Funds are deposited into Coach House’s operating bank account  
* Donation is recorded as **Coach House charitable revenue**  
* Revenue is assigned to the project’s **restricted fund**  
* Administrative fee is automatically calculated and recorded

### **4\. Donor Acknowledgment**

Coach House will:

* Issue an official tax acknowledgment to the donor  
* Retain donor records for compliance and reporting

Grantees **may not issue tax receipts** or guarantee deductibility.

**III. Ongoing Bookkeeping & Record‑Keeping**

Coach House maintains:

* A **separate restricted fund ledger** for each project  
* Transaction‑level detail including:  
  * Date  
  * Donor (if disclosed)  
  * Amount  
  * Restrictions  
  * Administrative fee  
  * Available balance

Monthly internal reconciliation includes:

* Bank reconciliation  
* Restricted fund balance review  
* Verification of outstanding obligations

**IV. Information Shared With the Grantee**

Coach House will provide the grantee with:

* Confirmation of each donation received (amount and date)  
* Periodic fund balance summaries  
* Notice of any donor‑imposed restrictions

Coach House does **not** share:

* Full donor tax documentation  
* Internal accounting system access  
* Other projects’ financial information

**V. Grant (Fund Release) Requests**

### **1\. What the Grantee Must Submit**

To receive funds, the grantee must submit a **written grant request** that includes:

* Amount requested  
* Intended use of funds  
* Expense category (e.g., program costs, supplies, consultants)  
* Timeframe for spending (within 90 days unless approved otherwise)  
* Supporting documentation, such as:  
  * Invoice or contract  
  * Budget line reference  
  * Explanation of relevance to approved project scope

### **2\. Review & Approval**

Coach House will:

* Confirm request aligns with approved project activities  
* Verify funds are available in the restricted fund  
* Review documentation for completeness and reasonableness  
* Approve, modify, or deny the request

Approval authority may vary by dollar amount.

**VI. Grant Disbursement**

Once approved:

* Coach House issues a **grant payment** to the legal entity on file  
* Payment is made via check or ACH  
* Disbursement is recorded as a **charitable grant expense**  
* Funds are removed from the project’s restricted balance

Grant payments are **not payroll, reimbursement, or vendor payments** made by Coach House.

**Coach House Fiscal Sponsorship – Grant Approval Authority**

To maintain appropriate financial controls and separation of duties, grant disbursement approvals follow the thresholds below:

* **Up to $5,000**  
   Approval by: Program or Finance Staff (designated)

* **$5,001 – $25,000**  
   Approval by: Executive Director (or designee)

* **Over $25,000**  
   Approval by: Finance Committee Chair or Treasurer  
   (Board notification required)

Additional approvals may be required if:

* The request falls outside the approved project scope  
* The project is under review or probation  
* Donor-imposed restrictions apply

**Coach House Donor Acknowledgment – Standard Operating Procedure**

### **Purpose**

To ensure accurate, timely, and compliant acknowledgment of charitable contributions, including restricted gifts.

### **Process**

1. **Donation Receipt**

   * Donation is received by Coach House and reviewed for acceptance  
   * Donor intent and project designation are confirmed

2. **Recording**

   * Donation is recorded as Coach House charitable revenue  
   * Funds are assigned to the appropriate restricted project fund

3. **Acknowledgment**

   * Coach House issues a written acknowledgment within 10 business days  
   * Acknowledgment includes:  
     * Donation amount and date  
     * Statement that no goods or services were provided (if applicable)  
     * Required IRS language  
   * No valuation is provided for non-cash gifts

4. **Restricted Gifts**

   * Donor restrictions are documented in the accounting record  
   * Grantee is notified of restriction terms

### **Important Notes**

* Grantees may not issue tax receipts  
* Grantees may not guarantee deductibility  
* All donor inquiries about tax treatment are referred to the donor’s tax advisor

## **VII. Post‑Disbursement Responsibilities**

### **Grantee Responsibilities**

The grantee must:

* Use funds solely for approved purposes  
* Spend funds within the approved timeframe  
* Maintain receipts and records  
* Provide reports upon request  
* Return unspent or misused funds

### **Coach House Oversight**

Coach House may:

* Request expenditure documentation  
* Conduct periodic reviews or audits  
* Suspend disbursements for noncompliance

**VIII. Reporting & Close‑Out**

Grantees are required to:

* Submit annual activity and financial summaries  
* Submit a final report upon termination

Coach House will:

* Reconcile remaining balances  
* Determine appropriate disposition of remaining funds  
* Issue final documentation

**IX. Key Control Principles**

* Coach House retains **variance power** at all times  
* All funds are legally Coach House assets until granted  
* Separation of duties is maintained between:  
  * Fund acceptance  
  * Accounting  
  * Grant approval  
  * Disbursement  
* Documentation is required at every stage

**X. Summary Flow**

Donor → Coach House (restricted fund) → Grantee (grant) → Program expenses

This process protects donors, sponsored projects, and Coach House while enabling effective community‑based work.
